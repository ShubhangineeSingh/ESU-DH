My research lies at the intersection of political geography, border studies, critical tourism, and memory politics in South Asia and Europe.

---

## Active Projects

### Project MEET: Memory, Everyday Bordering Experiences and Tourism

> **Funding Agency:** Estonian Research Council (ETAG)  
> **Position:** Principal Investigator / Postdoctoral Research Fellow  
> **Timeline:** 2025 – Present  

Project **MEET** examines the complex interplay of cultural memory and state-led geopolitical strategies. It investigates how leisure spaces and state boundaries converge in contemporary borderlands, transforming volatile frontiers into zones of managed heritage and political tourism.

## Ethnographic Fieldwork & Concepts

### Atmospheric Bordering (Eastern Himalayas)

> **Field Site:** Tawang, Arunachal Pradesh (India-China Borderlands)  
> **Theoretical Focus:** Volumetric geopolitics, altitude, and battlefield tourism  

Drawing on intensive ethnographic fieldwork in high-altitude border zones, I am developing the conceptual framework of **"atmospheric bordering."** This research explores how non-human elements—specifically altitude, terrain, weather, and air—interact with human agency and are shaping ongoing battlefield tourism initiatives and territorialization practices.
